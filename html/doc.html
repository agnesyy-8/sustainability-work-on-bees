BeeLikePro

Bee part of the community

How we came to this idea?

Our question of research was “Halt the loss of biodiversity”, which is one of the UN’s SDGs. We started the work by identifying the problems of biodiversity and how we can participate in reducing this issue digitally. In the beginning of research we found out that most of solutions posted on the web are NGOs raising funds for certain initiations done in person.

We wanted to come up with innovative solution addressing this issue head on.

We discovered that bees are pollinators that support the growth of trees, flowers, and other plants. This means they play a huge role in the continuation of biodiversity.

Thus, we wanted to create something accessible for beekeepers to encourage them to keep on taking care of bees and to improve the quality of bees’ life.

Ideas Brainstorming

how we manage the project selection process...

Our next step was collecting the ideas and solutions offered. During the brainstorming session we discussed several proposals and chose the idea of designing an application that connects beekeepers looking for advice with professionals offering help.
We narrowed down the targeted users to French beekeeping community. To make it more realistic we added payment feature to incentivize professionals to use the application.
To sum it up, on our online platform, the beekeepers will be able to register to this application and contact professionals to ask for advice on their current problems concerning bees. They would open the application on their phones and pick the professional that they would want to chat with and send him/her pictures and videos to visualize the problem. The professionals will receive the messages and have to reply within a span of 2-3 days with the solution or can ask more questions to pinpoint the problem first. This way the user would get best practices and solutions to deal with the bees to save their lives or improve their efficiency.

Process of development

Draft Wireframe

Next, we created a wireframe to describe the user flow and illustrate the location of the content. This is the first draft of how our application would look like.Initially, the functionality of the app provides users an opportunity to search for FAQ and chat with professionals. After some concerns were raised, we decided to create 2 separate user flows – one for a beekeeper and one for a professional, which later was renamed to “BeeHealer”.

Then, we started to create the mockups of the app to show the design choices for color, layouts and the visuals of navigation, as well as the overall perception of the product. It represents the realistic perspective of what the application will turn into after development.

Our user tests

After that we started with the testing phase to evaluate the design and usability of our application.
There were 2 categories in our testing phases – BeeKeepers and BeeHealers. During the interviews they reported several issues regarding the user experience. We took this into consideration and fixed the following:

1. Split recommendation page into two separate pages, where on the first one the users select the category of the problem and on the seconds there are the recommended BeeHeelpers (to make it more clear and user friendly)


2. Added pop-up that displays FAQ detailed answer (so that the user do not need to navigate to another page)
